Book Distribution: Unlocking Krishna's Mercy & Advancing Rapidly

üìÖ November 22, 2023 üìñ SB 7.9.44 üé§ Jai Sacinandana K·πõ·π£·πáa DƒÅsa

Key Takeaway

Distributing Srila Prabhupada's books is the most pleasing service to Krishna, offering unparalleled spiritual advancement and protecting one from material contamination, while fulfilling the highest welfare activity.

Main Theme

The Supreme Importance and Urgency of Distributing Srila Prabhupada's Books

Summary

Jai Sacinandana Krishna Das emphasizes the transformative power of book distribution, revealing it as the most direct and potent method for spiritual advancement. He inspires devotees to overcome excuses and embrace this essential service for personal and global spiritual upliftment, citing Prahlad Maharaj's compassion.

Key Concepts

The Compassion of Pure Devotees

Prahlad Maharaj exemplifies the highest compassion by desiring to deliver all fallen souls, not just achieving his own liberation. Unlike many saintly persons who seek solitary deliverance, Vaishnavas actively engage in spreading Krishna consciousness, even in challenging environments like material cities or malls. This selfless desire to bring others to Krishna's lotus feet defines a true devotee's mood and service.

Examples:

  • Prahlad Maharaj's prayer to Lord N·πõsi·πÅhadeva (SB 7.9.44).
  • Devotees going to malls during Christmas time to distribute books, seeing it as an opportunity to help souls.
  • Prabhupada's Guru's instruction: 'If you have life, preach.'

Protection and Elevation Through Sankirtana

Engaging in Krishna's service, especially book distribution (sankirtana), provides profound spiritual protection. A Krishna conscious person can remain happy even in 'hellish' places like shopping malls, because their consciousness is fixed on the Lord. Prabhupada assures that one cannot be contaminated on sankirtana if they follow basic regulative principles, being situated 'transiently' in the spiritual world even while physically in the material one.

Examples:

  • The speaker's personal experience of Krishna providing for his material needs (shoes) while on sankirtana.
  • Prabhupada's teaching that there's no difference between devotional service and the Kingdom of God.
  • The understanding that preaching is the safest and best activity.

Spreading Krishna Consciousness: The Highest Welfare

Krishna declares that one who preaches the message of Bhagavad-gita to His devotees is most dear to Him, and no one can excel him in satisfying the Lord. Spreading Krishna consciousness is the supreme welfare activity because it addresses the root cause of suffering‚Äîlack of spiritual awareness‚Äîunlike temporary material relief efforts. Pleasing Krishna by engaging in this service is the ultimate goal of life, as it guarantees everything else.

Examples:

  • The account of two individuals having out-of-body experiences, being told by God to 'bring them to Me'.
  • The BBT's work in printing books at affordable prices, making them accessible in 'the second most religious country in the world' (America).
  • The speaker's assertion that book distribution is the greatest opportunity for spiritual advancement in any kalpa.

Cultivating a Dutiful (Madhyama Adhikari) Mood

True spiritual progress requires moving beyond a 'fair-weather friend' approach to Krishna (Kanishtha Adhikari) and cultivating a dutiful, 'Madhyama Adhikari' mindset. This involves engaging in service to Guru and Krishna regardless of one's fluctuating physical or psychological conditions, recognizing these as material. The DUTY acronym (Desire, Unqualified, Tool, Your Holy Name) summarizes the mood of surrender and faith required.

Examples:

  • The example of not going out for sankirtana because one doesn't 'feel like it' or isn't inspired by a particular kirtan/class.
  • The speaker's personal 'DUTY' acronym (Desire for others' deliverance, feeling Unqualified but trusting Krishna, being Krishna's Tool, having faith in Your Holy Name).
  • The realization that 'it takes more energy to come back from a slack position to the top to that state than if I just maintain myself at that state'.

Intelligent Distribution and Overcoming Challenges

While the aim is to 'make books fly,' distribution should be intelligent, ensuring books reach receptive hands. Doubts about distributing to non-believers who might disrespect books are addressed by Prabhupada's instruction to 'flood the planet with books,' as even minimal contact can transform a soul (e.g., finding a book in the trash). The speaker also shares an anecdote of a 'Pingala moment' where surrendering to Krishna's will led to renewed enthusiasm and successful distribution.

Examples:

  • The speaker's own experience of throwing away his first Bhagavad-gita, yet eventually becoming a devotee and book distributor.
  • Nandakirtan Prabhu finding a 'Perfection of Yoga' book in the trash and becoming a devotee who distributed 300,000 books.
  • The story of the little cowboy boy who helped the speaker regain his loving feeling for distribution.
  • Prabhupada's statement that even appreciating a book distributor can lead to a human birth.

Maintaining Spiritual Enthusiasm and Optimal Time Utilization

Regular reading of Prabhupada's books is paramount for maintaining spiritual urgency and understanding. One's 'busyness' is often a choice, and there are countless ways to integrate Krishna consciousness (like listening to audiobooks during daily tasks). The concept of a 'stretch zone' encourages consistent, moderate effort to avoid both laxity and burnout, leading to sustainable spiritual growth. Condensing sadhana involves infusing every moment with Krishna's remembrance.

Examples:

  • Listening to Prabhupada's audiobooks while driving, showering, or before bed.
  • The analogy of the vehicle exhaust test, where one must maintain a precise 'stretch zone' of RPMs for success.
  • Hanuman Pashak Swami's analogy of filling a jar with sand, then pebbles, then water, demonstrating that there's always space for more spiritual practice.
  • The idea that 'Krishna is time' and having Krishna means having unlimited time.

How to keep the mood of distributing books intact continuously, especially if not doing it daily?
To maintain the mood, one must constantly increase their understanding of what these books are about, which comes from reading them daily. Prabhupada said that if we are not taking up this mission of sharing, we haven't understood it. Reading Bhagavatam helps understand the urgency, as every sentence implicitly asks to be distributed. Chanting attentively also helps. Make a point of distributing at least one book (even a small pamphlet) daily to 'stay in the fire.' If the urgency wanes, it means one needs to read Prabhupada's books more.
Should we be generous with books, letting them 'fly' even if no payment? How to balance passive vs. aggressive distribution?
Intelligent distribution means making the best use of energy. There are no hard and fast rules; sometimes giving a whole stack for free to an interested person is better than pushing one book on someone who will throw it away. The goal is that books are covered and BBT debt is managed. As for approach, distribute in a way that makes you want to distribute again tomorrow. Find your 'stretch zone' where you are challenged but not overwhelmed. Whether that's passive or aggressive, if it's productive for you and sustainable, it's okay. If nothing is happening from being passive, try a more aggressive approach. If being too aggressive is draining or ineffective, find a middle ground. The main thing is to find your comfortable and productive zone.
What about giving books to non-believers who might not respect them or even throw them away?
Prabhupada's instruction was to 'flood the planet with books,' even in wartime. There are countless stories of people finding books in trash cans and becoming devotees (e.g., Nandakirtan Prabhu). Even if someone throws it away (like the speaker did with his first Gita), that initial contact can be the beginning of their Krishna consciousness. While one shouldn't force books on people to the point of anger, giving them a chance is crucial. Any contact with the book is beneficial, even if they just walk by and appreciate the distributor, they are guaranteed a human birth.
Tips to motivate ourselves to read Srila Prabhupada's books regularly without fail, even when busy?
Realistically, we are as busy as we want to be. There is always time if one makes it. You can listen to audiobooks of Prabhupada's works while driving, showering, or before bed. Even short bursts of reading (one minute here, five minutes there) add up. If you find your urgency waning or consciousness getting disturbed, it's a clear sign you need to read more. Reading Prabhupada's books is like taking a 'pill' for the spiritual disease. Life is short, so utilize intelligence to make time for spiritual growth. Condense your sadhana by infusing every daily activity with Krishna consciousness; for example, chanting during mundane tasks. When Krishna (time) is added to your life, you have unlimited time.
How to overcome the competitive mentality when distributing books as a couple with small children, where one partner might sacrifice more time for childcare?
Part of your service as a householder is raising Krishna conscious children. It's not about neglecting everything else for book distribution; it's about finding a balance and not feeling guilty. Consider the 'big picture': if you can raise your child to become a book distributor, that's a huge service. Prabhupada didn't only distribute books himself; he engaged his disciples. It's a 'cultural conquest' where many services contribute. Don't be fanatical to the point of neglecting other duties, but if you have to lean one way, lean towards spiritual effort, as we tend to be too lax. Put everything in its proper perspective.
How should a book distributor think about distributing only Srila Prabhupada's books versus other devotional books?
This is a personal matter between the individual and Krishna. There's no dogmatic way. Some devotees distribute books by other authors (e.g., Vaisesika Dasa's 'Four Questions'). However, the late Prahlad N·πõsi·πÅha Prabhu, a prolific distributor, attributed his success to distributing only Prabhupada's books, citing Prabhupada's particular desire for certain books (especially hardbacks) to be distributed, as they guarantee one becomes Krishna conscious. Ultimately, it's about being true to oneself and what works best, but Prabhupada's specific instructions should be given great weight.
