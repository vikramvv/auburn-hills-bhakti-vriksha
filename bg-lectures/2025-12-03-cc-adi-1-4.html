Key Takeaway

This lecture underscores that book distribution, performed with faith and desire to please Krishna, is the topmost devotional service, offering unparalleled mercy and direct experience of spiritual ecstasy, requiring strategic yet sincere effort from the distributor.

Main Theme

The Supreme Mercy and Potency of Book Distribution in Kali Yuga

Summary

Jai Sacinandana Krishna Dasa highlights book distribution as the ultimate act of mercy for Kali Yuga, granting the highest devotional mellow. He shares Prabhupada's techniques: flatter, persist, and rely on the books' potency, emphasizing distributors' self-realization and need for Goswami's mercy.

Key Concepts

Lord Chaitanya's Unprecedented Mercy

Lord Chaitanya appeared in Kali Yuga to bestow the most sublime and radiant mellow of devotional service (conjugal love), never before offered by any incarnation. This opportunity is available even to the most fallen souls, without prior qualification, solely by causeless mercy. This stands in contrast to the traditional eligibility requirements for devotional service mentioned in scriptures like Bhagavad-gita.

Examples:

  • Rupa Goswami's blessing in CC Adi 1.4, praying for the Lord to be situated in the heart and bestow this mellow.
  • Prabhupada's statement, 'I have created you a good fortune,' indicating that eligibility is by mercy, not qualification.

Sankirtana in the Mood of the Gopis

≈örƒ´la PrabhupƒÅda confirmed that book distribution embodies the conjugal mood (the gopis' mood) because its ultimate goal is to please Krishna by engaging conditioned souls in devotional service. This highest level of service often requires personal sacrifice ('Sankirtana Jyogya') of bodily and mental comfort, but the reward is immense spiritual ecstasy and reciprocation.

Examples:

  • Prabhupada's letter confirming Ramaswar Maharaj's explanation that Sankirtana is like the gopis engaging others in Krishna's service.
  • The speaker's experience of 'swimming in ecstasy' on book distribution despite cold weather and rejection.

Foundation of Book Distribution: Rules and Study

The true 'technique' for successful book distribution is a solid spiritual foundation. This involves strictly following regulative principles (chanting 16 rounds and adhering to the four regulative principles) and deeply studying ≈örƒ´la PrabhupƒÅda's books. A strong internal spiritual life ensures the external service is powerful and sustainable.

Examples:

  • Prabhupada's conversation with Tripurari Maharaj in Atlanta (1975), where he affirmed, 'Our only technique is to be very devout followers of the rules and regulations.'
  • The analogy of building a house on a strong foundation, where 50-70% of the work is in the unseen foundation.

Tactics: Flattery, Humility & Persistence

Following the footsteps of predecessor acharyas like Prabodhananda Saraswati and ≈örƒ´la PrabhupƒÅda, using genuine compliments and a humble approach (e.g., 'My only humble request') is a bona fide technique. This helps to flatter people, disarm their resistance, and open the door for them to accept spiritual knowledge, encouraging them to set aside previous misconceptions.

Examples:

  • The speaker's common openers: 'That is what I call a hairstyle!' or 'You look a rock star!' to engage strangers.
  • Prabhupada's advice to flatter people, even 'falling down on his leg and becoming humble,' to encourage them to consider Chaitanya Mahaprabhu's teachings.

'Not Cheating': Strategic Approach to Mercy

Employing pleasing words or strategic tactics (like flattering) in Krishna's service is not deceitful or cheating. PrabhupƒÅda explained it as a mother's trick to give medicine to a sick child. The ultimate goal is to engage someone's mind in Krishna consciousness (yena tena prakƒÅre·πáa mana·∏• k·πõ·π£·πáe nive≈õayet), and any means that achieves this, within ethical bounds, is justified for their spiritual benefit.

Examples:

  • Prabhupada's analogy of a mother promising 'luglulu' (candy) to get a child to take medicine.
  • The principle of 'whatever you got to do' to bring someone to Krishna consciousness, emphasizing the ultimate purpose over rigid adherence to conventional honesty in initial interactions.

Potency of All Books & Self-Realization

All of ≈örƒ´la PrabhupƒÅda's books are imbued with transcendental potency, regardless of their complexity. There's no hierarchy; even a basic book can profoundly impact a new person. Furthermore, PrabhupƒÅda stated that distributors who dedicate themselves to this service are already self-realized, as their intense labor demonstrates their love for Krishna and their conviction in His supremacy.

Examples:

  • The 'sugar cane' analogy: 'Anywhere you touch it is sweet' to illustrate that all books are potent.
  • The speaker's personal experience of being transformed by 'Teachings of Lord Chaitanya' despite not fully understanding it.
  • Prabhupada's statement to Tripurari: 'You are already self-realized. Otherwise, how you can push on the books?'

Seeking Mercy of the Six Goswamis

Obstacles and objections are natural in the mission of spreading Krishna consciousness. To overcome these impediments and achieve success, it is vital to seek the mercy and shelter of the Six Goswamis of Vrindavan. Chanting prayers like ≈örƒ´ Sadgoswami Astaka is a powerful practice that guarantees protection and success in one's devotional endeavors.

Examples:

  • Krishna Das Kaviraj Goswami's prayer (CC Adi 1.3-4) to the Six Goswamis for assistance in his writing.
  • The speaker's personal experience during a book distribution marathon in Sydney, where chanting prayers to the Six Goswamis transformed his results and mood.

Practical Sales Psychology & Persistence

Effective book distribution involves understanding human psychology and employing strategic sales techniques. This includes identifying 'ripe fruit' (receptive individuals), offering limited choices (e.g., 'extra or basic cost'), skillfully negotiating, and maintaining persistence with charm and a smile. Avoiding arguments and focusing on the transaction is key to efficiency.

Examples:

  • The 'ripe fruit' analogy from grapefruit picking: only engage those who are receptive and show immediate interest.
  • The sales close: 'Can you do a bit extra or can you just cover the basic cost of printing?' to guide people toward a donation.
  • The speaker's observation of a partner who breaks through multiple 'no's' with persistence and charm.
