Key Takeaway

Krishna declares that upon attaining His service, a devotee experiences such complete satisfaction and unwavering conviction that no other gain or difficulty can disturb their spiritual steadiness.

Main Theme

Finding supreme satisfaction and unshakable steadiness in Krishna's service

Summary

This lecture elaborates on Bhagavad Gita 6.22, explaining how true satisfaction comes from attaining Krishna's service. By developing unshakable faith (≈õraddhƒÅ) in devotional activities, one achieves a state where no material gain seems greater, and no suffering can cause agitation, leading to supreme fulfillment.

Key Concepts

The Nature of Ultimate Gain (Labham)

The lecture differentiates between temporary material gains and the ultimate spiritual gain. Material achievements, no matter how grand, often leave one still hankering, insecure, or jealous. In contrast, the 'labham' described in BG 6.22 refers to the attainment of Krishna's service, which brings such complete satisfaction that one feels there is nothing more to be achieved, nor any desire for 'what next'. This is the defining characteristic of realizing the Absolute Truth.

Examples:

  • Comparing achieving wealth or marriage to achieving Krishna; only the latter eliminates jealousy or further hankering.
  • The feeling that 'I got that labham' but still observing jealousy in oneself as an indicator that it wasn't the supreme gain.

Supremacy of Sevanubhuti (Experience of Service)

The speaker argues that even higher than 'atmanubhuti' (self-realization) or 'moksha' (liberation) is 'sevanubhuti'‚Äîthe experience of rendering service to the Lord. While j√±ƒÅnƒ´s and those who achieve liberation may experience transcendental pleasure, they still find joy in Hari (Krishna), indicating that the ultimate pleasure and fulfillment lie in engaging in His devotional service. True perfection is not just realizing one's spiritual identity but actively serving the Supreme Lord.

Examples:

  • Jnanis also find pleasure in Hari, suggesting Sevanubhuti is higher than Atmanubhuti.

Unshakable Steadiness in Devotion

One who has attained this ultimate spiritual gain (Krishna's service) becomes 'yasmin sthito na du·∏•khena guru·πáƒÅpi vicƒÅlyate'‚Äîunshaken even by the gravest difficulties. This state of complete steadiness and freedom from agitation comes from the realization that Krishna's lotus feet are the highest objective. When one is fixed in this conviction, all material gains or losses, respect or disrespect, cease to have any significant impact on one's inner peace and spiritual focus.

Examples:

  • Prahlada Maharaj, Mucukunda, Shrivasa Thakur, and Vishwanatha Chakravarti Thakur are examples of devotees who achieved this supreme, unshaken situation.

The Essential Role of Shraddha (Faith)

'Shraddha' (firm faith and conviction) is presented as the foundational element required both to achieve and to maintain this supreme spiritual state. This means having an unflinching belief that engaging in devotional activities like chanting Hare Krishna or studying Bhagavad-gƒ´tƒÅ is the absolute highest activity one can perform. This 'parab shraddha' (supreme faith), combined with understanding Krishna's supreme position (Hari sarvottamaha) and the sacredness of everything related to Him (tadiyya), enables one to focus wholeheartedly on sadhana and realize the ultimate goal.

Examples:

  • One must have the conviction that chanting Hare Krishna is the highest activity to truly engage in Krishna consciousness.
  • Feeling the 'paratvam' (supremacy) of the Lord and everything associated with Him.

BG 6.22: Prabhupada's Most Important Shloka

The speaker reveals that Srila Prabhupada considered BG 6.22 to be the most important verse in the entire Vedas. This is because understanding and embodying the principles of this verse‚Äînamely, the supreme satisfaction found in Krishna's service and the unwavering faith it engenders‚Äîprovides the fundamental conviction necessary to understand and practice all other spiritual teachings. It explains *why* one should engage in devotion and what the ultimate reward truly is.

Practical Applications

  • Cultivate unwavering faith in devotional service (Shraddha)
  • Discern ultimate gain from temporary achievements
  • Appreciate the supremacy of selfless service (Sevanubhuti)

Notable Quotes

"If you have felt jealous after having your gain, then whatever you have achieved is not me. That is not spiritual."
"If at all you have achieved me, this is the meaning of God. This is the meaning of absolute truth."
"If that question comes [what next?], that means we have not realized Krishna."
"Sevanubhuti is the highest. To achieve the service of the Lord is the highest."
"Yam labdhwa. You've achieved something, but there is no more hankering for anything more. There is no more jealousy. There is no more insecurity, complete satisfaction, complete fulfillment, complete confidence that there is nothing more and complete realization that all of the things in this world are an illusory potency of the Lord."
"Unless one feels this [chanting Hare Krishna is the highest activity], it's not possible to actually engage in Krishna consciousness."

"He is fixed in the realization. Realization of what? The lotus feet of the Lord is the highest objective to achieve."
"All my activities can be stopped if I have achieved the lotus feet of the Lord. There is nothing to do in this world. There is nothing to achieve. There is nothing to gain. There is nothing to lose. There is no respect to gain. There is no respect to lose. There is no money to gain. There is no money to lose. There is no fame to be achieved. No fame to be lost."
"If I can have darshan of Krishna's lotus feet, if I can fall at his lotus feet and beg at his lotus feet for a little service, this is the highest situation of my life."
"In that service, in that realization that I am of some use to him, some insignificant use to him lies my supreme situation, lies my supreme fulfillment. There is nothing more that I need to achieve outside of it."
"Service of spiritual master is called God. This is God."
"When one feels the supreme faith arising in one's heart, this is called parab shraddha."
"Prabhupada said this is the most important shloka of the entire Vedas."
"When you understand the Hari parataha, Hari sarvottamaha, when you understand the sarvottamatvam of Hari and everything that is tadiyya... then that bhakti, that parabhakti... must come."
"When we feel that is the supreme thing, when we feel that sense of confidence that I am doing the supreme thing, then we can very easily focus on our sadhana."
