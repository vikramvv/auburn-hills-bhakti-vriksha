Key Takeaway

Krishna Consciousness is the easiest and most natural process of yoga, enabling one to transcend material senses, perceive Krishna's presence everywhere, and achieve liberation from fear and anger through devotional service.

Main Theme

Achieving Krishna consciousness through devotional service

Summary

This lecture explains Krishna Consciousness as the most natural and easiest yoga, freeing one from fear and anger. By perceiving Krishna's presence in everything, devotees transcend material senses and control desires effortlessly through devotional service.

Key Concepts

Transcendental Situation vs. Sensual Situation

The lecture elaborates on how Krishna consciousness operates in a 'transcendental situation,' meaning it goes beyond ordinary sensual perception. Unlike mental or sensual experiences that are temporary and forgettable, realizing Krishna's presence in a transcendental state cuts across all boundaries of bodily, mental, and intellectual understanding, providing a constant and unwavering connection. This state is not an artificial imposition but the natural inclination of the living entity.

Examples:

  • Transatlantic refers to crossing the Atlantic; similarly, transcendental means transcending the senses.
  • A car seen independently of Krishna represents materialistic consciousness, whereas realizing its connection to Krishna is transcendental.

Krishna Consciousness as the Natural Position

Srila Prabhupada emphasizes that Krishna consciousness is the natural position of the living entity, not an artificial imposition. This means the inherent urge for love and connection, present in every being, finds its true fulfillment only in a relationship with Krishna. Forgetting this innate connection leads to suffering and entanglement in the material world of names and forms, but by realizing Krishna's true identity, one can resume their original, natural spiritual situation.

Examples:

  • The natural earning for love in every species.

Supremacy of Bhakti for Sense Control

The lecture presents Krishna consciousness as a superior and easier method for controlling the senses compared to Ashtanga Yoga. Instead of forcefully suppressing senses, a Krishna conscious person naturally achieves sense control by realizing Krishna's presence within their heart and in everyone and everything. This realization automatically pacifies the senses, just as children become quiet when a principal passes by, leading to freedom from material desires like lust, fear, and anger.

Examples:

  • Children shrieking become quiet when a principal passes by, illustrating automatic sense control.
  • Controlling lust and anger by becoming conscious of Krishna, rather than direct suppression.

Krishna as the Absolute Truth and Inseparable Source

Krishna is described as the Absolute Truth, the source of all existence, and therefore inseparable from anything that exists. The lecture argues that seeing anything as independent of Krishna is a form of materialistic consciousness or even a 'dream world.' True realization ('tatvataha') involves understanding that nothing in this world is separate from Krishna. This understanding purifies one's vision and makes Krishna consciousness a natural state rather than a laborious effort.

Examples:

  • Seeing a car as existing independently of Krishna is materialistic consciousness.
  • Nothing in this world existing independently from Krishna.

Practical Means: Service and Chanting

The most effective way to realize Krishna in reality is through the process of bhakti, characterized by love and service. The speaker specifically emphasizes chanting the Hare Krishna Maha-mantra (Hare Krishna, Hare Krishna, Krishna, Krishna, Hare Hare, Hare Rama, Hare Rama, Rama Rama, Hare Hare) with great earnestness, an attitude of service, and eagerness. This sincere calling out to the Lord, who is the innermost entity of all, causes Him to reveal Himself and reside permanently in the devotee's heart.

Examples:

  • Calling out to Krishna through chanting.

Transcendence of Material Desires

When one becomes fully conscious of Krishna, material tendencies such as desire (ichha), fear (bhaya), and anger (krodha) automatically lose their grip. These negative tendencies only have a place when one is unconscious of Krishna. The speaker uses the analogy of shadows: shadows exist only when one turns their back to the sun; similarly, bad tendencies exist only when one is not facing Krishna consciousness. Aroused Krishna consciousness nullifies these negative forces.

Examples:

  • The analogy of shadows disappearing when facing the sun.
  • The disappearance of lust and anger when Krishna consciousness is aroused.

Role of Pure Devotee Association

The dormant Krishna consciousness within every living entity can be awakened and expanded through the association of a pure devotee. Just as a desire for worldly things can be aroused by certain stimuli, the dormant spiritual consciousness is aroused and nurtured in the company of those who are fully absorbed in Krishna. This association helps one to become 'drowned in pools of existence,' reuniting with the Lord and expanding spiritual awareness.

Examples:

  • The arousal of sexual desire in a young man/woman upon seeing the opposite sex, compared to the arousal of dormant Krishna consciousness.

  • Awaken dormant Krishna consciousness through devotional service.
  • Sharpen intelligence through submission to spiritual authority.
