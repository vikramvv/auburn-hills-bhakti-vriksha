Key Takeaway

Controlling the mind by relinquishing material designations and cultivating humility through constant obeisances is the devotee's austerity, leading to Ruchi and Nistha in Krishna Consciousness.

Summary

This lecture on BG 6.7 emphasizes that true mind control comes from shedding material designations and offering constant obeisances. This humility, the devotee's austerity, melts attachments and allows one to easily experience spiritual progress and attraction for Krishna.

Key Concepts

The Mind as a Burden Carrier

The mind carries a heavy burden due to its attachment to various material and even spiritual designations. These designations (such as family, country, job, or even roles like 'preacher' or 'book distributor') create false identifications, leading to artificial happiness, sadness, and agitation. This attachment to external roles weighs down the mind, making it difficult to achieve true control and inner peace. Just as one feels relief after removing heavy jewelry, the mind feels light when freed from these self-imposed burdens.

Overcoming Material and Spiritual Designations

To control the mind effectively, one must consciously shed all designations. This includes not only obvious material roles but also spiritual identities if they are approached with a material mindset. When one performs spiritual service while expecting recognition or becoming agitated by lack of cooperation, the 'spiritual' designation itself becomes a burden. The goal is to purify the intention behind all actions, especially during core practices like chanting the Hare Krishna mantra, by momentarily relinquishing all perceived roles and positions and embracing the position of a humble servant.

The Austerity of Humility (Namanta Yeva)

In Krishna Consciousness, the primary austerity for mind control is not physical hardship like meditating in a desert or enduring extreme weather, but rather cultivating humility through constant obeisances. The phrase 'Namanta Yeva' (simply by offering obeisances) emphasizes this central practice. Offering obeisances to the Lord, the spiritual master, and the devotees breaks down ego and material designations, melting them away like snow in the sun. This act of humble surrender makes one attractive to Krishna and facilitates easy mind control, especially during chanting. Brahmaji's prayers in the Srimad Bhagavatam also highlight the ease with which householders can progress by simply offering obeisances.

Spiritual Progress: Nistha and Ruchi

The controlled mind, achieved through the austerity of humility and shedding designations, leads to tangible spiritual progress. The immediate result of a controlled mind is 'Nistha,' or steadiness and firm faith in devotional service. Once Nistha is established, a devotee naturally develops 'Ruchi,' a genuine taste and attraction for Krishna Consciousness. This progression from mind control to steadiness, and then to a heartfelt taste for devotional activities, is a clear indicator of spiritual advancement and absorption in the Supersoul (Paramatma samahitaha).
