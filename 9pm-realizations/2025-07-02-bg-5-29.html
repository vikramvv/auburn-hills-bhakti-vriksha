Main Theme

Realizing Krishna as the Supreme Enjoyer, Controller, and Friend

Summary

This lecture on BG 5.29 elucidates that genuine peace and success stem from knowing Krishna as the ultimate enjoyer, controller, and friend. It contrasts this with material ignorance, highlighting how focusing on Krishna frees one from life's embarrassing struggles and offers real spiritual gain.

Krishna Consciousness vs. Material Ignorance

Krishna consciousness means working with full knowledge of one's eternal relationship with Krishna as His fragmental part and parcel, always His servant. The opposite is working in full ignorance, believing oneself to be the body and denying God, leading to the illusion of personal gain and the conviction of living forever in a temporary world. This ignorance is the absence of Krishna consciousness.

Examples:

  • Devotees distributing books daily, working long hours, taking baths in rivers or Walmarts, driven by knowledge of Krishna's position.
  • People absorbed in 'personal agendas,' confidently believing they will live forever in a temporary body.

Spiritual Mathematics: The Paradox of Gain and Loss

The lecture introduces 'spiritual mathematics,' explaining that in material life, when one thinks they are gaining materially, they are actually losing spiritually. Conversely, when one endeavors to benefit Krishna, they gain immensely. True benefit comes from making Krishna the benefactor, challenging the illusory mathematics of the material world where self-satisfaction leads to suffering.

Examples:

  • "As soon as we think we are gaining materially, we start losing."
  • "As soon as we want to benefit Krishna, we gain."

Escaping Material Embarrassment and Illusion

The material world is described as 'embarrassing' due to the constant frustration of attempts to enjoy, compared to the trickery of a Turkish ice cream seller. Despite constant setbacks (anxiety, fear, insults), people act as if they are successful, failing to recognize their embarrassed state. Krishna consciousness frees one from this embarrassment by shifting focus from personal enjoyment to serving Krishna, thereby embracing His lotus feet.

Examples:

  • Turkish ice cream seller analogy: customer repeatedly denied ice cream, left feeling foolish.
  • Material 'success' (promotion, vacation) hides 'thousands of slaps' from anxiety and fear.

True Success and the Urgency of Spiritual Life

The lecture asserts that there is no true success or gain in the material world because all material possessions (gross or subtle) are temporary and will eventually be lost or cause embarrassment. The only real success is to take to Krishna consciousness, realize Krishna's position, and come close to Him. The speaker emphasizes the urgency of not wasting this valuable human life by associating with non-devotees and instead reading Srimad Bhagavatam and taking Srila Prabhupada's instructions seriously.

Examples:

  • Any material gain (money, pride, respect) will eventually go away or lead to embarrassment.
  • "Whatever can leave us, will anyway leave us for sure."
  • Srila Prabhupada 'cried' in Bhagavatam purports about the urgency of Krishna consciousness.
