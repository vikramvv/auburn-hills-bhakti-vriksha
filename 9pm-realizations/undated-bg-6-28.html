Krishna's Touch: Finding Fearless, Endless Happiness

Key Takeaway

Krishna declares that genuine, fearless, and endless happiness (Brahma Samsparsham Sukham) is attained by constantly connecting with Him, detaching from temporary material pleasures, and overcoming anxieties caused by fleeting worldly attachments.

Main Theme

Achieving fearless, everlasting happiness through Krishna consciousness

Summary

This lecture explains that true happiness, termed Brahma Samsparsham Sukham, comes from purifying our consciousness and constantly connecting with Krishna. By detaching from temporary material attachments that cause anxiety and fear, we can experience a fearlessness and joy that is independent of changing circumstances.

Key Concepts

The Nature of Brahma Samsparsham Sukham

The lecture introduces 'Brahma Samsparsham Sukham' as the transcendental contact with Krishna, which automatically brings happiness. It contrasts this with 'Maya Samsparsham' or 'Moha Samsparsham,' where our contact with material forms, sounds, and objects leads to anxiety and temporary pleasures. The core idea is that connecting with Krishna directly leads to inherent and eternal joy, as opposed to the fleeting and pain-inducing contact with matter.

Examples:

  • Touching Krishna (Brahma Samsparsham) leads to happiness.
  • Touching matter (Guna Samsparsham) leads to anxiety and fear.

Material Happiness vs. Spiritual Happiness

The speaker meticulously dissects the limitations of material happiness. He points out that even the highest material pleasure is temporary, lasts only for a few minutes, and is fraught with anxiety. It leads to attachment, and consequently, pain when separation inevitably occurs (e.g., from body, relationships, possessions). Spiritual happiness, or Brahma Samsparsham Sukham, is presented as 'Atyantam Sukham,' an unending happiness, independent of material circumstances.

Examples:

  • Sexual happiness, considered the highest material pleasure, lasts only 2-3 minutes.
  • The desire for new cars, houses, vacations creates successive, but ultimately illusory, happiness.
  • Fear of death or old age arises from attachment to the body as a source of happiness.

Process of Purification and Constant Connection

The lecture emphasizes that to 'touch' Krishna and experience Brahma Samsparsham, one must become purified. This purification is achieved by constantly engaging in activities that connect one to Krishna. It's a continuous process where consistent engagement with Krishna leads to freedom from material contamination ('kalmasam') and an ever-increasing state of joy.

Examples:

  • To become purified, one must constantly touch Krishna.
  • Freedom from 'kalmasam' (misery/dirt) is a result of this constant connection.

Addressing Fear and Attachment

A significant part of the lecture focuses on how fear serves as an indicator of attachment to temporary, material sources of happiness. When we fear losing something (a job, a relationship, our health), it reveals our dependence on that external factor for happiness. A practicing devotee is advised to be attentive to this fear, understanding that it signals an attachment to something that is not the Absolute Truth and can be taken away.

Examples:

  • Fear of death is due to attachment to the body as a source of enjoyment.
  • Fear of losing a country or a company stems from seeing them as primary sources of happiness.
  • Adi Shankaracharya's observation that old people are 'cintamagnaha' (submerged in anxiety) due to their inability to enjoy sensually.

Negation (Neti Neti) as a Path to Krishna

The speaker suggests using a process of negation to identify what is not Krishna. If an object or relationship causes fear upon the anticipation of separation, it is not the Absolute Truth. Conversely, what does not cause fear and is free from 'kalmasam' (misery) must be God or connected to God. This helps in training oneself to become independent of material affiliations in the search for true happiness.

Examples:

  • If an attachment causes fear, it's not the Absolute Truth.
  • Whatever brings happiness without fear or misery must be God.

Spiritual Training for Old Age

The lecture stresses the importance of preparing spiritually before old age sets in. As one's bodily senses weaken, reliance on sensual enjoyment leads to moroseness and discouragement. By training oneself in spiritual happiness and detaching from material life by a certain age (e.g., 50 years as per Vedic tradition, referencing Rishabhadeva), one can cultivate a state of fearlessness and inner joy independent of physical decline.

Examples:

  • Vedas suggest retiring from family business at 50 years (panchashordham varambarajit).
  • Rishabhadeva going to the forest with his wife as an example of early detachment.

"As soon as we touch Krishna, Sukhayaena, we become very happy."
"We can touch Krishna when we become purified. And how do you become purified? We have to constantly touch Krishna."
"Katha means there is no other kalmasam, there is no other misery, there is no other dirt."
"This Sukham, this Brahma Samsparsham Sukham, this Sukham is not this Sukham Antam, Anta Sukham."
"The problem with material happiness is that this new, new, new happiness that we are trying to achieve is illusory. It is written with so much anxiety and also it will end."
"During the course of happiness, we develop attachment to the cause of happiness. And when we develop attachment... then there is pain or there is anticipation of separation."
"Any person whose definition of enjoyment is sensual will become very discouraged, will become unenthusiastic, will become very morose when he becomes old."
"Before we become old, we must train ourselves with spiritual happiness. We must disassociate ourselves from all material happiness."
"We have to analyze whether our happiness is dependent on material circumstances or independent of material circumstances."

"Whatever I am attached to that doesn't cause fear, that must be God."
