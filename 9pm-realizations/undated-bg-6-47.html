Transform Your Mind: Master Senses for Krishna Bhakti

Key Takeaway

Achieving true sense control and realizing one's spiritual identity comes not just from mechanical practices like pranayama, but through active surrender and fervent begging for Krishna's mercy, thus transforming the mind for His service.

Main Theme

Achieving true sense control through active devotion to Krishna

Summary

This lecture explores the profound journey of sense control, contrasting mechanical Ashtanga Yoga with the devotional approach of Bhakti Yoga. It emphasizes turning the mind inward for Krishna, recognizing our spiritual identity, and actively seeking Krishna's mercy to overcome material attachments and truly engage our senses in devotional service.

Key Concepts

The Inward Turn: Discovering Spiritual Identity

The mind, when absorbed in external sense objects, leads to a dissipated and constantly changing identity, making it difficult to know the real self. By turning the mind completely inward, away from outward sensations, one can purify their identity and clearly distinguish the soul from the material body. This realization is likened to evaporating water from a sweet or extracting oil from a seed, revealing the true essence. This initial understanding of the soul's distinction from matter is called Brahman realization, compared to the sunlight illuminating the sky before the sun (Krishna) has fully risen in the heart.

Examples:

  • Sugary sweet mixed with water (obscuring true sweetness)
  • Ghee and milk, or oil within a seed (requiring processing to reveal essence)

Pratyahara: Mechanical vs. Devotional Sense Control

Ashtanga yoga employs mechanical processes like Pratyahara (withdrawal of senses) and Pranayama (breath control) to arrest the outward tendency of the mind and senses, similar to caging a bird in a net. An Ashtanga yogi or a Jnani renounces material enjoyment primarily because it causes pain. However, this mechanical suspension of agitation is temporary. A Bhakti-yogi also practices sense control, but their renunciation is rooted in a desire to offer themselves as the 'bhogya' (enjoyed object) to Krishna, the 'bhokta' (supreme enjoyer), rather than merely avoiding pain. This transforms the basis of renunciation into a loving offering.

Examples:

  • Patanjali's comparison of catching a bird in a net to controlling senses via pranayama
  • Tylenol as a temporary suspension of pain, similar to pranayama's temporary suspension of agitation

Ahara and Bhogya: The Nature of Enjoyment

The term 'Ahara' traditionally refers to food, but in a broader sense, it means enjoyment, as the senses 'eat' or absorb their respective objects (eyes 'eat' forms, ears 'eat' sounds). Material life is characterized by the individual 'eating' or enjoying sense objects. In Krishna consciousness, this concept is completely reversed: the devotee aspires to be the 'bhogya' (the enjoyed object) for Krishna, who is the supreme 'bhokta' (enjoyer). The highest consciousness involves recognizing oneself as the 'food' being eaten by Krishna, marking a complete shift from selfish enjoyment to selfless offering.

Examples:

  • Senses 'eating' (enjoying) forms, sounds, smells, tastes, and sensations
  • The concept of 'aha mannam' where the devotee becomes the enjoyed object for Krishna

The Superiority of Bhakti & Active Surrender

While mechanical processes like Pranayama can temporarily suspend agitation, they are not sustainable. True and lasting sense control comes from love for Krishna, developed through attentive hearing from a pure devotee. Such hearing awakens affection for Krishna, which is crucial for internalizing the Hare Krishna mantra and directing senses inward. If affection doesn't arise, it indicates either duplicitous tendencies or not hearing from a genuine source. Genuine surrender means actively redirecting the senses towards Krishna, not passively allowing them to wander, realizing that 'one cannot become a yogi' if one is still hankering for sense objects.

Examples:

  • Rukmini Devi understanding Krishna by hearing about Him
  • The prayer 'bhajahure mana shri nandanandanam' (Oh mind, worship Krishna)

The Urgency of Begging Krishna for Advancement

Most individuals do not possess 'rati' (spiritual attachment) from previous births, making their current spiritual advancement inadequate and even dangerous. Therefore, it is imperative to intensely and consistently beg Krishna and the spiritual master for help in controlling the senses and fixing the mind on the Lord. One should not become complacent or proud about their spiritual situation, as such a mindset can lead to spiritual stagnation or fall down. Active 'vignyapti' (humble petition) is essential for receiving divine mercy and progressing in Krishna consciousness.

Examples:

  • The speaker's personal experience of not having rati from previous births
  • The warning against spiritual pride or idleness, which can lead to spiritual regression
